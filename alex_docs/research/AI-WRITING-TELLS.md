# AI-Generated Writing Is Everywhere, and It's Still Easy to Spot—For Now

Artificial intelligence–generated writing is flooding the internet, and once you notice it, it's impossible to ignore. It's creeping into inboxes, slipping into publications, and giving the web a sense of déjà vu—as if we've been here before with another wave of low-quality content. Tech watchers even turn it into a guessing game: what percentage of online content do you think is created by machines now? Forty percent? Maybe ninety in the near future? Whatever the number, the rise feels relentless.

---

## The Six Categories of AI Tells

### 1. Vocabulary Tells (The Biggest Giveaway)

Even though AI bots are getting better at mimicking human patterns, their writing still carries the unmistakable scent of the lab. Vocabulary is usually the first sign. Chatbots write like panicked college freshmen trying a little too hard to sound profound.

#### Red Flag Words by Category

| Category | Words to Avoid |
|----------|----------------|
| **Academic Flair** | delve, myriad, plethora, multifaceted, nuanced, paradigm |
| **Poetic Overreach** | tapestry, beacon, mosaic, symphony, landscape, realm |
| **Trendy Jargon** | liminal, holistic, synergy, leverage (as verb), optimize |
| **Empty Intensifiers** | crucial, vital, essential, pivotal, paramount, indispensable |
| **False Precision** | specifically, notably, particularly, fundamentally, inherently |
| **Corporate Speak** | cutting-edge, state-of-the-art, innovative, robust, seamless |
| **Transition Padding** | furthermore, moreover, additionally, consequently, thus |

My personal favorite? **Liminal**—a word ChatGPT insists the wider culture overused first. Touchy, isn't it?

### 2. Structure Tells

Another hallmark of AI-generated prose is its compulsive need to announce its intentions. Instead of diving into an argument or telling a story, it starts with a preamble:

> "In this article, we will explore…"

It's the verbal equivalent of a nervous student raising their hand and explaining how they'll answer the question before they actually do.

**Common structural patterns:**
- **The Preamble**: Announcing what the document will cover
- **The Sandwich**: Intro → exactly 3-5 points → restating conclusion
- **The Enumeration Addiction**: Compulsively numbering everything

### 3. Tone Tells

AI is terrified of being wrong, so it hedges everything:

| AI Hedging | Human Directness |
|------------|------------------|
| "It's worth noting that..." | (just say the thing) |
| "One might argue that..." | "Some people think..." |
| "It could be said that..." | (delete and state directly) |
| "In some cases, it may be..." | "Sometimes..." |
| "It's important to consider..." | (just consider it) |

AI also writes like a diplomatic robot: no strong opinions, no personality, no humor, no admission of uncertainty.

### 4. Phrase Tells

These phrases almost never appear in natural human writing:

- "In the ever-evolving landscape of..."
- "Navigating the complexities of..."
- "Unlock the potential of..."
- "A testament to..."
- "Serves as a beacon..."
- "Resonates deeply with..."
- "In today's world..." (it's always today)
- "When it comes to..." (just start with the topic)

### 5. Content Tells

**Missing specificity**: AI gives generic examples because it can't verify real ones.

❌ *"Many companies have found success with this approach."*
✅ *"Acme Corp tried this last quarter and cut costs by 12%."*

**No personal experience**: AI can't draw from lived experience.

**Balanced to a fault**: AI presents perfectly balanced arguments on everything, never committing to a position.

### 6. Format Tells

- **Bullet addiction**: Everything becomes a list
- **Predictable headers**: Generic, parallel structure
- **Rule of Three**: Always exactly three points, three examples, three recommendations
- **Over-organization**: Sub-sub-sections for everything

---

## The Consequences

The consequences of this shift are already taking shape:

- **Email threads** swell with bland, over-explained updates
- **Online articles** risk losing their distinct voices, leaving readers unsure whether a human or a bot is speaking
- **Trust erodes** when everything starts to sound like a polite corporate intern or a jittery undergrad

People stop believing that what they're reading carries genuine thought or experience.

## Why This Is Happening

The background to this surge is simple: AI tools are everywhere.

| Use Case | Appeal | Cost |
|----------|--------|------|
| Companies churning out blog posts, marketing material, internal memos | Speed | Authenticity |
| Students relying on them for essays and assignments | Efficiency | Learning |
| Social media managers maintaining constant activity | Volume | Quality |

## Looking Ahead

Right now, spotting AI writing is still easy if you know the tells. But as the technology improves and bots learn to hide their quirks, distinguishing human voices from automated ones will get harder.

If we're not careful, the internet could become an endless hall of mirrors, reflecting machine-written echoes back at us.
