## Strategy 0: Collaborate with Doctoral Supervisor (PRIORITY)

### Why This Is Your Best First Move

Your doctoral supervisor is the **highest-probability collaboration path**:

| Advantage | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Existing relationship** | No cold outreach; she already knows your work quality |
| **Invested in your success** | Your publications reflect well on her and the program |
| **AI expertise alignment** | Direct match with Alex's core contribution |
| **Ongoing access** | Regular meetings, not one-off interactions |
| **Network multiplier** | Can introduce you to her collaborators and venue editors |
| **Resource access** | Potential student help for user studies |

### Best Article Versions for AI-Focused Supervisor

Given her AI focus, prioritize these versions:

| Priority | Version | Venue | Why |
|----------|---------|-------|-----|
| **1st** | #10 Agents | NeurIPS Workshop | Core AI/LLM agents topic |
| **2nd** | #04 CogSci | Cognitive Systems Research | AI + cognitive architecture |
| **3rd** | #01 HCI | CHI | Human-AI interaction |
| **4th** | #09 Ethics | FAccT | AI ethics angle |

### Conversation Approach

Since you have an existing relationship, this is a conversation, not a pitch:

```
"I've been working on a side project called Alexâ€”a VS Code extension that
gives AI coding assistants persistent memory. It's been running for 18
months and I think there's a publishable contribution here.

I'd love your advice on:
1. Which AI venues would be strongest for this work
2. Whether you'd be interested in collaborating on a publication

I have draft papers ready targeting NeurIPS workshops, Cognitive Systems
Research, and CHI. Would you have time to look at one and give me your
thoughts?"
```

### Questions to Discuss with Her

1. **"Does this fit with your current research agenda?"** - If yes, stronger motivation
2. **"What AI venues would you target?"** - She knows acceptance patterns
3. **"Would you want to be a co-author, or would acknowledgment be appropriate?"** - Let her choose
4. **"Do you have students who might help with a user study?"** - Multiplies resources
5. **"Is there an angle that connects to your ongoing work?"** - Creates mutual benefit

### What to Prepare Before the Conversation

- [ ] Have arXiv pre-print ready (shows seriousness)
- [ ] Prepare 1-page summary of contributions
- [ ] Know which venues match her publication history
- [ ] Have specific ask ready (co-author vs. advisor vs. introducer)

### Potential Contribution Levels

| Her Contribution | Author Position | Likely Scenario |
|------------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Strategic framing + venue selection + edits | 2nd author | Most likely |
| Light feedback + introduction to editors | Acknowledgment | Also valuable |
| Substantial rewriting + theoretical grounding | 2nd author | Best case |
| Connects you with her PhD students for user study | Co-author or Acknowledgment | Bonus |

### Timeline

| When | Action |
|------|--------|
| **This week** | Post #10 to arXiv (shows you're serious) |
| **Next meeting** | Have the conversation with her |
| **Within 2 weeks** | Clarify collaboration terms if interested |
| **Within 30 days** | Submit first joint paper |

### If She Declines Co-Authorship

Still valuable outcomes:
- Ask for **feedback** on the draft
- Ask for **venue recommendations**
- Ask for **introductions** to researchers in her network
- Ask if her **students** might be interested in collaborating

## Strategy 4: Conduct User Study

### Why User Studies Matter

Academic venues (especially CHI, CSCW) strongly prefer empirical validation. Even a small study dramatically strengthens submissions.

### Minimum Viable Study Design

**Participants**: 10-15 professional developers

**Duration**: 2-4 weeks of Alex usage

**Data Collection**:

- Pre-survey: Current AI assistant usage, pain points
- Usage logs: Memory accumulation, command usage
- Post-survey: Perceived value, trust, knowledge retention
- Interviews: 3-5 participants, 30-minute semi-structured

**Metrics to Report**:

| Metric | Measurement |
|--------|-------------|
| Context re-establishment time | Self-reported reduction |
| Memory inspection frequency | Log analysis |
| Trust development | Likert scale over time |
| Knowledge persistence value | Qualitative themes |
| Net Promoter Score | Standard NPS question |

### Recruitment Channels

| Channel | Approach | Expected Response |
|---------|----------|-------------------|
| VS Code Marketplace | In-extension opt-in | Low volume, high quality |
| Twitter/X | Post seeking beta testers | Medium volume |
| Reddit r/vscode | Community post | Medium volume |
| Dev.to | Article with study recruitment | Low-medium volume |
| Personal network | Direct outreach | High conversion |
| University CS departments | Student participants | High volume, variable quality |

### User Study Timeline

| Week | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 1 | Prepare consent forms, surveys, interview guide |
| 2 | Recruit participants (target 20 for 15 completions) |
| 3-6 | Participant usage period |
| 7 | Post-survey and interviews |
| 8 | Data analysis |
| 9-10 | Write up findings |

**Action**: Begin recruitment within 60 days if targeting CHI 2027 (September deadline).
