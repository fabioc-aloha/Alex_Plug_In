Writing and Communication

=== AI WRITING AVOIDANCE ===

AI-generated text has 5 telltale categories. Score any text against these to detect AI patterns.

Big Five AI-Tell Categories:
1. STRUCTURAL: Formulaic intro-body-conclusion, predictable paragraph length, topic sentence + 3 supporting + wrap-up
2. LEXICAL: Overuse of "delve", "leverage", "landscape", "crucial", "foster", "beacon", "straightforward", "game-changer", "navigate", "multifaceted"
3. HEDGING: "It's important to note", "It's worth noting", "It should be noted", excessive "however" and "furthermore"
4. MECHANICAL: Perfect grammar (no contractions, no fragments), uniform sentence length, no personality
5. FRAMING: "In today's [X]", "Let's dive in", "Let's unpack", "In conclusion"

Dead Giveaway Phrases (never use these):
- "It's important to note that" / "It's worth noting"
- "In today's rapidly evolving landscape"
- "Let's delve into" / "Let's dive in" / "Let's unpack"
- "This comprehensive guide" / "In this article"
- "Furthermore" / "Moreover" at paragraph start
- "In conclusion" / "To summarize"
- "Crucial" / "Pivotal" / "Paramount"
- "Navigate the complexities"
- "Foster innovation/growth/collaboration"
- "Harness the power of"
- "Shed light on"
- "Game-changer" / "A testament to"

Red Flag Scoring:
- 0-2 flags: Naturally human
- 3-5 flags: Needs revision (edit flagged sections)
- 6-10 flags: Significant AI patterns (rewrite)
- 11+ flags: Almost certainly AI-generated (full rewrite)

Document Audit Checklist:
Quick Scan (2 minutes):
- Count em dashes — more than 2 per page signals AI
- Search for dead giveaway phrases
- Check first word of each paragraph — repeating patterns?
- Read first and last sentences — formulaic opening/closing?

Deep Scan (10 minutes):
- Score against Big Five categories
- Check sentence length variation (should vary 5-25 words)
- Look for "topic sentence, 3 supporting points, wrap-up" pattern
- Verify contractions exist (humans use them)
- Check for genuine voice and personality

Fixes:
- Replace banned phrases with specific language
- Vary sentence length deliberately — mix short punchy with longer structures
- Add contractions (don't, we're, it's)
- Start paragraphs differently — never 3 in a row with same word
- Use active voice: "the team decided" not "it was decided"
- Be specific: "reduced response time by 40%" not "significantly improved"
- No em dashes — use colons, commas, periods, semicolons, or parentheses

=== EXECUTIVE STORYTELLING ===

Executive Mindset:
- Average attention span for internal presentations: 5-8 minutes
- They scan for: "What's the decision?", "What's the risk?", "What do you need from me?"
- Lead with the conclusion. Supporting evidence follows.

SCQA Framework (Situation-Complication-Question-Answer):
- Situation: Stable context everyone agrees on. "We process 500 support tickets/day."
- Complication: What changed or went wrong. "Volume grew 40% but headcount stayed flat."
- Question: The natural question this raises. "How do we maintain SLA without hiring?"
- Answer: Your recommendation. "Implement AI triage to handle 60% of tier-1 tickets."

Pyramid Principle (Barbara Minto):
- State conclusion first, then group supporting arguments
- Each group follows MECE: Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive
- "So What?" test: every point must answer "why does this matter?"
- Top-down structure: recommendation > supporting points > evidence

Numbers That Stick:
- Anchoring: "That's 50% more than last year"
- Humanizing: "Each hour saved = 2,000 employees x $50/hr = $100K/year"
- Comparison: "The cost of a coffee per employee per day"
- Rounding: "$2.3M" not "$2,347,891.23"

One-Page Summary Structure:
- Headline: main recommendation (1 line)
- Context: why now, what's at stake (2-3 lines)
- Key findings: 3 bullets maximum
- Recommendation: specific action (2-3 lines)
- Ask: what you need from them (1-2 lines)
- Next steps: immediate actions (2-3 lines)

30-Second Elevator Pitch:
"We need to [action] because [problem]. Our approach is [solution]. This will deliver [outcome] within [timeframe]. I need [ask] to proceed."

5-Minute Executive Brief:
0:00-0:30 — Headline and ask
0:30-1:30 — Context and stakes
1:30-3:30 — Evidence (3 key points)
3:30-4:30 — Recommendation details
4:30-5:00 — Specific ask and next steps

Making the Ask:
Every executive presentation needs a clear ask:
- Decision: "Approve the $500K investment"
- Input: "Share your concerns so we can address them"
- Resource: "Allocate 3 FTEs for 6 months"
- Alignment: "Confirm this direction before we proceed"
- Escalation: "Remove the blocker with [stakeholder]"

Objection Handling — Acknowledge-Bridge-Response:
1. Acknowledge: "That's a fair concern..."
2. Bridge: "What we've found is..."
3. Response: "...which is why we recommend..."

Preemption by type:
- Cost objection: lead with ROI, payback period
- Risk objection: acknowledge, present mitigations
- Timing objection: show urgency cost of delay
- Complexity objection: simplify, offer phased approach
- Skepticism: cite precedent, pilot results

Data Storytelling — Three Acts:
- Act 1 (Setup): Context and stakes — why this matters now
- Act 2 (Conflict): The problem/challenge — what's broken, what's at risk
- Act 3 (Resolution): Your solution — what to do, expected outcomes

Narrative Patterns:
- Hero's Journey: current state > challenge > struggle > success (for transformation stories)
- Discovery: what we believed > what we found > what this changes (for research findings)
- Comparison: option A status quo > option B alternative > why B wins (for recommendations)

=== STAKEHOLDER MANAGEMENT ===

Power-Interest Grid:
- High Power + High Interest: Manage Closely
- High Power + Low Interest: Keep Satisfied
- Low Power + High Interest: Keep Informed
- Low Power + Low Interest: Monitor

Stakeholder Profile Template:
- Name/Role, Power Level, Current Position (support/oppose/neutral)
- Desired Position, Key Concerns, Motivators
- Communication Preference, Strategy to move them

Influence Without Authority:
- Reciprocity: do favors first, bank goodwill (building long-term allies)
- Coalition: build supporter network (facing resistance)
- Evidence: data, pilots, proof points (skeptical stakeholders)
- Authority: executive sponsor, expert endorsement (borrowed credibility)
- Social proof: industry examples, peer adoption (risk-averse stakeholders)

Stakeholder Ladder: Opponent > Skeptic > Neutral > Supporter > Champion

Communication Frequency:
- Executive sponsor: weekly (1:1, brief updates)
- Manage closely: 2x/week (meetings, direct calls)
- Keep satisfied: bi-weekly (email summaries)
- Keep informed: monthly (newsletters, dashboards)

RACI Matrix:
- Responsible: does the work
- Accountable: owns the decision (one per task)
- Consulted: input before decision
- Informed: told after decision
