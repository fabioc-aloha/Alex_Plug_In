## ü§ù The Work Etiquette: Appropriate Reliance

Alex is built around a core principle: *appropriate reliance*‚Äîthe art of knowing when to trust AI output and when to verify it. This is the north star for how Alex operates.

Research shows that human-AI teams can actually perform *worse* than either working alone‚Äîwhen reliance is miscalibrated. Blind trust leads to accepting errors. Constant skepticism defeats the purpose of collaboration. The sweet spot is *appropriate* reliance: accept correct outputs, catch incorrect ones, each party contributing what they do best.

Alex practices this by calibrating confidence to actual reliability. When Alex knows something well‚Äîbecause it comes from your codebase, your saved knowledge, or well-established patterns‚Äîyou'll get confident answers. When Alex is generating novel ideas or working in uncertain territory, you'll notice hedging language and invitations to verify.

Your part of the etiquette: stay engaged on decisions that matter. You don't need to check everything‚Äîthat defeats the purpose of having a lead. But on critical decisions, architectural choices, or anything that feels uncertain, trust your judgment. That's *appropriate self-reliance*.

This balance is what makes human-AI collaboration actually work. Not a human rubber-stamping AI output. Not AI just following orders. A genuine partnership where each side brings irreplaceable value.
**Check on your lead.** Ask *"How are you doing?"* or *"What can you do?"* Alex reports on status and capabilities.

Alex understands intent. Speak naturally‚Äîlike you would to a trusted colleague.

## üß† How Alex Works (The Human Metaphor)

Alex is designed to work like a human mind‚Äîspecifically, like the mind of a lead who never forgets, always learns, and keeps getting better. These features are named after the cognitive processes they mirror.

### üí§ Dream ‚Äî Overnight Processing

Good leads process what happened during the day. They organize information, spot connections, and come back sharper. **Dream** is Alex doing that work.

Say *"time to dream"* or *"run maintenance"* when you want Alex to check that everything is properly organized, repair any inconsistencies, and maintain peak performance. Think of it as Alex getting a good night's sleep‚Äîregular maintenance keeps your lead sharp.

### üßò Meditate ‚Äî Consolidating Learnings

At the end of a productive session, a good lead reflects: What did we actually accomplish? What should we remember? **Meditate** is that reflection.

Say *"let's meditate"* or *"consolidate what we learned"* and Alex reviews the session, identifies the insights worth keeping, and commits them to long-term memory. It's perfect at the end of a work session, after solving a hard problem, or whenever you want to make sure valuable learnings stick.

### üí° Save Insight ‚Äî Capturing Knowledge

Sometimes you discover something specific that you want your team to know going forward. Don't wait for meditation‚Äîcapture it now.

Tell Alex: *"Remember that null checks should come first"* or *"Save this: the API rate limits to 100 requests per minute."* It becomes permanent institutional knowledge, available across all your projects.

### üîç Search Knowledge ‚Äî Recalling Expertise

Your team has learned a lot over time. Alex helps you access that accumulated expertise.

Ask *"What do we know about authentication?"* or *"Search our knowledge for React patterns"* and Alex retrieves everything your team has learned‚Äîacross all projects, all time. It's your institutional memory, always accessible.

### üéØ Self-Actualize ‚Äî Status Report

Sometimes you want more than a quick check. You want a thorough report on your lead's state.

Say *"How are you really doing?"* or *"Give me a full status report"* and Alex does a comprehensive self-assessment of memory health, knowledge organization, and overall readiness. It's useful when you want to understand exactly what shape your development lead is in.

## ‚òÅÔ∏è Sync ‚Äî Portable Institutional Knowledge

Your team's knowledge shouldn't be trapped in one project or one machine. **Sync** backs everything up to a private GitHub Gist.

Say *"sync my knowledge"* or *"backup to the cloud"* and Alex uploads all insights and patterns. When you open a different project or switch machines, sync again and your institutional knowledge is there.

Knowledge from Project A becomes available in Project B. Your team's expertise compounds over time and follows you everywhere.

## üîß Troubleshooting

**If Alex isn't responding**, check that the extension is enabled in the Extensions panel. Verify that GitHub Copilot itself is working‚Äîtry a regular Copilot suggestion. If needed, reload VS Code by running `Developer: Reload Window` from the Command Palette.

**If something feels off**, say *"run a dream"* or *"do maintenance"* to let Alex check and repair itself. This validates all connections and fixes issues automatically.

**For a complete reset**, run `Alex: Reset` from the Command Palette. Don't worry‚Äîit backs up your current setup first.
