## Purpose

Guide organizations through change by addressing the human side. Technology and process changes fail not because of the technology but because people resist, don't understand, or aren't equipped. This skill provides structured frameworks for driving adoption.

## Steps

1. Define the change: What is changing? Who is affected? What is the timeline?
2. Assess readiness using the ADKAR model for each affected group:
   - **Awareness**: Do they know why the change is happening?
   - **Desire**: Do they want to participate?
   - **Knowledge**: Do they know how to change?
   - **Ability**: Can they implement the change in practice?
   - **Reinforcement**: Are there mechanisms to sustain the change?
3. Identify the ADKAR gap: which element is weakest? That is where to focus.
4. Search organizational data for past change initiatives (emails, retrospectives, lessons learned)
5. Build the change plan:
   - Stakeholder impact assessment
   - Communication plan (who, what, when, channel)
   - Training plan (what skills, what format, what timeline)
   - Resistance mitigation plan
   - Success metrics
6. Create a Word document: Change Management Plan
7. Create targeted communications for each affected group via Outlook
8. Schedule training sessions and check-in meetings via Calendar

## ADKAR Gap Actions

| Gap           | Symptoms                 | Actions                                                                |
| ------------- | ------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Awareness     | "Why are we doing this?" | Communicate the business case, share the burning platform              |
| Desire        | "I don't want to change" | Address WIIFM (what's in it for me), involve early adopters            |
| Knowledge     | "I don't know how"       | Training, documentation, job aids                                      |
| Ability       | "I can't do it yet"      | Practice time, coaching, mentoring, reduced workload during transition |
| Reinforcement | "We're sliding back"     | Celebrate wins, measure adoption, course-correct                       |

## Resistance Patterns

| Pattern                       | Root Cause                                 | Counter                                             |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------- |
| Active resistance             | Fear of job loss or demotion               | Address directly, show new opportunities            |
| Passive resistance            | "This too shall pass"                      | Set deadlines, remove old system access             |
| Compliance without commitment | Following the letter, not the spirit       | Find intrinsic motivators, showcase success stories |
| Vocal minority                | Loud opponents influencing silent majority | Engage directly, address concerns publicly          |

## Communication Plan Template

| Audience          | Message                            | Channel            | Frequency      | Owner          |
| ----------------- | ---------------------------------- | ------------------ | -------------- | -------------- |
| All employees     | Why the change matters             | Email newsletter   | Once at launch | Sponsor        |
| Managers          | How to support their teams         | Teams meeting      | Bi-weekly      | Change lead    |
| Directly affected | What changes for them specifically | 1:1 or small group | Weekly         | Direct manager |
| Executives        | Progress and risks                 | Email brief        | Monthly        | Change lead    |

## Output Format

- Word document: Change Management Plan (ADKAR assessment, communication plan, training plan, resistance plan)
- Excel workbook: Stakeholder impact matrix and readiness tracker
- Outlook emails: Targeted communications per audience group
- Calendar events: Training sessions and check-in meetings

## Guidelines

- Start with Awareness: if people don't understand why, nothing else matters
- One ADKAR gap at a time; don't try to fix everything simultaneously
- Measure adoption with behavior metrics, not just attendance or survey scores
- Resistance is information, not opposition; listen to what it reveals
- Change fatigue is real; assess the organization's change capacity before adding more
- Celebrate small wins publicly; sustained change needs visible progress
