name: Literature Review
description: Conduct systematic literature searches, synthesize findings, identify research gaps, and construct evidence-based narratives.
---

## Purpose

Conduct a rigorous review of existing knowledge on a topic. Identify what has been studied, what the findings say, where experts agree and disagree, and what gaps remain. Produce a structured synthesis document suitable for academic papers, business reports, or strategic decisions.

## Steps

1. Define the research question precisely: what specific question does this review answer?
2. Establish inclusion/exclusion criteria: what types of sources count? (academic, industry, date range, geography)
3. Conduct systematic search using Deep Research across multiple source types
4. For each relevant source, extract:
   - Citation (author, year, title, publication)
   - Key findings (2-3 bullet points)
   - Methodology (how they studied it)
   - Limitations (what they acknowledged as gaps)
5. Organize findings by theme, not by source (thematic synthesis)
6. Identify patterns: where do multiple sources agree? Where do they disagree?
7. Identify gaps: what has not been studied? What questions remain open?
8. Write the synthesis as a Word document:
   - Introduction (research question, scope, criteria)
   - Thematic sections (findings organized by theme)
   - Agreements and disagreements (cross-source analysis)
   - Gaps and future directions
   - Reference list
9. Create an Excel companion: source tracking matrix with columns for citation, findings, methodology, relevance rating

## Source Evaluation

| Criterion   | Strong                               | Weak                                    |
| ----------- | ------------------------------------ | --------------------------------------- |
| Recency     | Published within 5 years             | Over 10 years old (unless foundational) |
| Peer review | Academic journal or conference       | Blog post or opinion piece              |
| Methodology | Stated and reproducible              | Methodology unclear or absent           |
| Sample size | Adequate for claims made             | Too small for generalization            |
| Relevance   | Directly addresses research question | Tangentially related                    |

## Synthesis Patterns

| Pattern     | What it means                                          |
| ----------- | ------------------------------------------------------ |
| Convergence | Multiple sources agree: high confidence finding        |
| Divergence  | Sources disagree: report both sides with context       |
| Gap         | No sources address this aspect: flag as open question  |
| Evolution   | Findings have changed over time: trace the progression |

## Output Format

- Word document: structured literature review (introduction, themes, gaps, references)
- Excel workbook: source tracking matrix
- Each finding cites its source explicitly
- Gaps listed as [OPEN QUESTION] with suggested research directions

## Guidelines

- Organize by theme, not by author (thematic, not annotated bibliography)
- Report disagreements honestly; do not cherry-pick sources that support one conclusion
- Always include limitations of the evidence base
- Separate what the sources say (findings) from what you conclude (synthesis)
- Aim for breadth first, depth second: cover the landscape before deep-diving
- Include a clear statement of the search strategy so others can reproduce it
